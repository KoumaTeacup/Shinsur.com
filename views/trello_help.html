Welcome to the IP Garden! Click on cards below for more info.

The IP Garden is a collaborative space for brainstorming our next big IP.

This Trello Board represents a new process in IP development at Insomniac:

The IP Garden.

It's a collaborative space where ideas can inspire other ideas and combine into exciting new forms, all in the interest of cultivating Insomniac's next big IP.

Contribute at whatever level you'd like! You can suggest ideas for game worlds, character stories, or game mechanics. You can add comments to an intriguing idea. Or you can add an image that inspires you.

Our collective ideas can transcend what we can conceive as individuals.

You can contribute Seeds for gameplay, world, story, innovation, or look ideas --

Seeds are modular ideas that combine to form new game concepts.

To plant a new Seed:

  1. Click the + Add a card option at the bottom of a column.
  2. Write your idea. For best results, use crisp, clear, active-voice syntax.
  3. Capitalize the key idea of the card so that it's easy to spot.
  4. Select your card and set a Label

Seeds should be modular; they can stand on their own, and don't require multiple cards to make sense.

Your Seed should be a new idea. If a similar card exists, consider enhancing it with comments instead of creating a new card.

There are five Seed categories:

  • CORE GAMEPLAY Seeds establish the foundation of your idea. What's the bread-and-butter gameplay?

  • WORLD Seeds describe the setting, genre, or tone. What makes the world compelling?

  • STORY Seeds describe the hero and their journey, similar to a logline for a film. What's the hero's goal? What's their obstacle? And how do they change?

  • INNOVATION Seeds propose ideas that make the core game experience fresh and distinctive. What makes the game exceptional? What does it do first or best?

  • LOOK Seeds describe the visual style of the game. This category demands art to illustrate what's compelling about the look. What's intriguing and beautiful about the look of the game?

Each column includes example cards to get the ball rolling.

-- Or you can combine Seeds into Sprouts.

Sprouts are combinations of Seeds that can grow into novel game concepts. Think of them as mini-pitches.

To grow a new Sprout:

  1. Press the + Add Another List button on the far right of the page.
  2. Drag your new column to the end of the Sprouts section.
  3. Name the Sprout.
  4. Copy and paste seeds into your Sprout column. please copy and paste the card to duplicate it; keep the original card in its Seed column.
  5. Add one or two Seeds from each category to develop your idea.

Ideas form a compelling whole in the Summary and Discussion card:

  1. Add the Summary and Discussion card to hold additional commentary.
  2. Write a few sentences as a mini-pitch in the Summary and Discussion card. There should be a core fantasy underlaying your seeds--do your best to articulate it!
  3. You can also add a Reference Media card for images, video, or any other content that brings the idea to life!

It's good to consider Insomniac's house style --

Insomniac and Sony are searching for a new IP in our "house style".

Use these guidelines to help drive ideation:

  • Action or Action-Adventure genre
  • 3rd-person camera
  • Story-driven single-player campaigns
  • Wide-linear or open-world level structure
  • AAA scale with moments of spectacle
  • Underdog heroes
  • Blockbuster potential
  • "Cinematic realism" visual style

-- But please share anything that inspires you!

Insomniac is always evolving, so there's room to push the boundaries. Feel free to let your imagination run wild as you ideate in the garden.

We can always bonsai-prune Sprouts to grow toward the house style when it's time to craft a resolved pitch.

Help us measure enthusiasm by adding comments or reactions.

You can add comments and reactions to cards!

To comment or react:

  • Select the card and share your thoughts in the Write a comment field. You can share images in comments, too.
  • Use the Reactions option on the right side of the card.

Use these options to offer feedback, generate new ideas, or share what you're most interested to make.

We'll use comments and reactions to help measure enthusiasm for ideas across the organization.

What are some effective ways to iterate on my pitch?

Developing a pitch is hard, and it takes several passes to iron out your ideas and sharpen your writing. Here are some tactics to iterate on your pitch and make it the best it can be.

  • Bug your (work) friends. Not only will they have fresh ideas to contribute, but they'll catch comprehension issues too.
  • Ping the IP channel. Use #ig-ip-garden in Slack to get others to read it and provide feedback. This is also a useful resource for getting notes from other disciplines to shore up ideas you're uncertain about.
  • Go back to the garden. Sometimes a seed might not be strong enough. If there's a conceptual weak point in your pitch, consider a stroll through the garden and see what fresh new ideas might be a better fit.
  • Delve further into this FAQ column!

How can I sharpen up my idea?

These seven "spices" can help you punch up a mild idea into something flavorful!

  • Familiar Fantasies: What situation does the player get to be in that they may have only seen or imagined in other media?
  • Payoff: What is fundamentally satisfying about the mechanical and psychological experience?
  • Reversals: How can/should we subvert expectations, given what will already be familiar to the player?
  • Spectacle: How can we push the envelope for technology and the player experience?
  • Stakes & Mysteries: What are the rules or dynamics of this world that players will be forced to reckon with?
  • Suspense & Cliffhangers: What story and character dynamics are in motion that the player will be able to influence?
  • The Unexpected: What did players not even realize they wanted or needed?

How else can I evaluate my idea?

The following four elements can help guide you in creating a new IP that demonstrates a compelling creative vision and has the best chance of success.

The Core Fantasy

What's the core concept of the game and why is it broadly appealing? This should be some kind of fantasy fulfillment that's easy to understand. Marvel's Spider-Man has the easiest elevator pitch: you get to be Spider-Man! With Ratchet, the player embarks on a space adventure through a colorful galaxy. Touch on the game's basic premise here, but you don't need much narrative at this stage of the creative process. If you're having a hard time explaining what makes your game compelling, you may need to land on a simpler and stronger core fantasy.

Innovation

What new ideas does your game bring that sets it apart? Perhaps it's a versatile new mechanic, or maybe it's a mash-up of existing concepts. For example, the game Bleeding Edge is Overwatch but with melee combat. Innovation can take other forms too; perhaps your game tells its story in a unique way. Your game doesn't need to be revolutionary, but it should have a strong hook to support a novel and fresh experience.

You can also take a broader picture of innovation: with an imaginative concept, your game will be fertile ground for the team to grow their own ideas. For example, Ratchet's longevity is in part due to its flexible premise that allows a variety of gameplay experiences to be creatively justified with each installment.

Again, focus on what makes your game innovative–we can assume it'll have other systems (like progression) to fill out the experience, but if they're not essential to the concept leave them out of the pitch.

If you're game is leaning too hard on established conventions, it may be lacking in gameplay innovation.

Personality

What qualities make your game playful, charming, or cool? Why do players want to visit this imaginative new reality? What makes your characters endearing, cute, or badass? What aspects of your game create moments of pure spectacle that leave players stunned and amazed? Go ahead and double-down on your game's weird idiosyncrasies--we don't want our games to feel generic.

Fit

Is your game more than the sum of its parts? Is it tonally consistent? Is there a type of gamer that it appeals to? Every element of your pitch should feel like it's essential to fulfilling that core fantasy–if there's too many ideas at play, consider cutting down or consolidating them so you're pitch is more focused and cohesive.

Additionally, your game should generally fall in the Insomniac / Sony house style--third-person action-adventure games that are narratively-driven and in a cinematic realism visual style. It's certainly okay, and even encouraged, to push at some of these boundaries–we are constantly evolving–but if it's too far out there your game might not be a good fit. Your game should also feel like it's a force for good–at this stage, a positive message is a plus, but at least avoid creating something distasteful or cynical.

What if I can't squeeze my big idea into a one-sentence Seed?

Everything big is made up of smaller pieces. Try to beak your idea down into the smallest components you can until you reach something that none of the other Seeds can touch. The remaining pieces that don't fit into your new Seed can always be combined to strengthen or evolve existing Seeds via the Comments section in each card.

What if I just don't like this process?

This is the beginning of a new inclusive kind of pitch process here at Insomniac. Our intention is to create a fun environment that produces viable ideas, with a balance of freedom, ownership, and quality.

If you feel that any part of this new process is not delivering on that promise, speak up, and we'll work together on making it better for everyone.

Can I pitch an existing IP?

Yes you can! This process is not strictly limited to original IP; you can pitch a sequel or reimagining to an existing Insomniac title! If you do, the process remains the same: build a Sprout out of Seeds to establish what makes the new game concept compelling.

For a refresher, Insomniac's own IP includes:

  • Sunset Overdrive
  • Ratchet & Clank
  • Edge of Nowhere
  • Feral Rites
  • The Unspoken
  • Stormland
  • Song of the Deep
  • Outernauts

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT?

We'll measure engagement.

Comments, reactions, and the volume of off-Trello discourse will help us understand how enthusiastic the studio is about an idea. That information will heavily influence the ideas we select for further development.

We'll discuss ideas on Slack.

We'll start up an IP Ideas Slack channel. There, we can dig deeper into ideas that have traction--or sharpen up ideas that need a little more work to get off the ground.

We'll select promising ideas.

We'll work together as a studio to identify ideas that seem especially promising--ideas that we're excited to make and play, that seem like they should exist in the world. There's no preconceived idea about the number; we'll let the process play out and see where we stand. If we end up with multiple ideas that have the potential to be unforgettable GOTY contenders--that's great! The conversation about our best path forward will make our 2022 pitches even stronger.

We'll hold opt-in, full-studio brainstorms.

After we've selected ideas for further development, we'll hold full studio brainstorms to capture more ideas. In this way, we'll benefit from focused, discipline-centric ideation modes. Everyone in the studio will have a chance to help shape the vision.

We'll create informal strike teams.

After group brainstorming, we'll form strike teams to answer important questions:

  • What's the core of this idea, and how can we make it even stronger?
  • What's the vision statement?
  • What are the pillars?
  • What story will we tell? What are we trying to say?
  • What are the technical opportunities and risks of this idea?

We'll factor this information into the publisher-facing pitch.

And finally, we'll create Sony-facing game pitches.

After we have a set of ideas with traction, and a strong sense of the core premise and innovation potential of each one, we'll craft game pitches.

In each pitch, we'll tell a story about the player experience, state the vision, outline the pillars, summarize the game story, and dig deep into gameplay systems, production implications, and potential cultural impact. We'll illustrate each pitch with incredible production art.

From there, we'll share with our partners at Sony. We might share a single idea that's a clear frontrunner, or we might share 2-3 ideas that have potential.

We'll identify the next big Insomniac IP in 2022, collaborate with our partners to greenlight, and move into the planning and team-building stage immediately after that.
